About

ACX brings traditional commodities trading infrastructure to the carbon markets. This means we offer LOW FEES, EFFICIENT TRADING, SETTLEMENT RISK MITIGATION AND TRANSPARENT PRICING.

ACX is a Global Carbon Exchange using distributed ledger technology on a traditional trading architecture. It leverages blockchain architecture to create securitized carbon credits.

The Past

The Rio Earth Summit in 1992 marked the first time the international community recognized climate change as a man-made phenomenon. From that point, countries began wrestling with potential solutions to the fast-approaching climate crisis. The Kyoto Protocol in 1997 was a landmark treaty through which countries agreed to establish a global cap-and-trade system to mitigate carbon emissions. Between 1997 and 2005, signatories grappled with how to deliver on their Kyoto promises, and in 2005, the Kyoto Protocol was ratified with one significant absence: the USA. The protocol divided countries into Annex 1 and non-Annex 1 countries. Europe and other developed nations were in the former category and emerging economies, including the BRICs, were in the latter. The market came to a halt on the heels of the global financial crisis. It was not until the Paris Accords in 2016 that we saw movement toward a new concerted effort to avert the climate crisis.

The Present

Today, with the Glasgow Climate Pact outlining a plan to achieve the reductions of the Paris Climate Accords, we have the beginnings of a new carbon market. Corporations, in response, have reinvigorated sustainability efforts—many setting ambitious carbon net-zero goals. For many/most corporations, the only immediate way to make progress on their sustainability goals is through investments in voluntary carbon credits to offset their current carbon use. As a result, the voluntary markets have scaled quickly, creating a robust carbon market—topping U$1bn in 2021. McKinsey & Company forecasts that global carbon credit market demand will increase by a factor of 15 or more, growing to U$50bn by 2030 (Source).

Our Contributon

In 2018, the founders of ACX discussed with Enterprise Singapore — the government agency championing enterprise development — the possibility of launching a digital carbon exchange in Singapore.

The AirCarbon Exchange was an offshoot of these conversations, and our original plan was to target the growing CORSIA (Carbon Offset Reduction Scheme for International Aviation).

We immediately realized that there was a much larger opportunity beyond just international aviation carbon offsets and quickly expanded across a broad carbon market offering.

As we began planning to develop the expanded ACX Exchange, we examined the current carbon credit market to identify weaknesses and inefficiencies.

Our goal was to build competitive advantages into the ACX Exchange, and we saw two exploitable issues.

First, financial intermediaries dominated carbon credit transactions, forcing sellers to incur large transaction fees, often up to 20-30%. Second, many of the carbon credit marketplaces relied on antiquated technology, for example, using bulletin boards to market and track carbon credit projects.

This created inefficiencies, requiring carbon credit sellers to navigate a multitude of marketing and administrative tasks to post, market, and sell projects.

We realized that if we could replicate what other commodities markets had done, using efficient technology underpinnings and establishing consistent pricing benchmarks for carbon credits, we could transform the market.

We explored technology innovation and concluded that blockchain was the right digital infrastructure to support an efficient exchange, to simplify and secure the process, and to offer new and innovative credit offerings that would differentiate ACX.

The central innovation introduced by ACX was the launch of the tradable standardized contract.

Since then, we’ve remained true to our notion of bringing traditional commodities architecture to the carbon markets.

Our Evolution

In 2022, we decided to evolve our message and brand to represent the broader opportunity. We transitioned from AirCarbon Exchange to ACX, establishing an ambitious company vision of creating harmony between sustainability efforts and the global economy.

We expanded our global reach with offices in Singapore, Abu Dhabi, London, and São Paulo. Ultimately, we broadened environmental credit coverage on our platform, enabling traders, project developers, and the broader carbon market to trade carbon and other environmental credits efficiently and seamlessly.

ACX provides global access for nations to establish local environmental credit exchanges.
